Angelica is a talented, well-traveled architect with a passion for art and design. She is originally from the Philippines and lived in different parts of the world, including Saudi Arabia, Italy and the United States. Angelica earned her Bachelor of Architecture degree at Philadelphia University with honors. She also attended the American University of Rome and the University of Santo Tomas in Manila. She combines creativity and technical expertise to produce solutions. Throughout her career she has exceeded clients expectations through quality design with schedule and budget in mind.

Angelica worked on several types of projects including luxury residential homes, U.S. embassies, restaurants, nursing facilities, and offices and has been in several publications such as "Greater Philadelphia House and Home Magazine" and the "Dream Homes Books."

In 2009 she moved to Texas and joined Ted Trout Architects & Associates as Project Manager. The projects she worked on included private and publicly-funded (HUD) multifamily/residential apartments, student housing, senior living facilities and commercial offices. In 2012, her design for the Chipolbrok Office Headquarters was a Houston Business Journal Landmark Award Finalist.

Angelica believes in giving back and enjoys serving her community. As a leader, she served as President of the Rotary Club youth division Interact Club. She was also chosen to represent the Philippines in Japan as an ambassador for the Asian Youth Program and was awarded by the Philippine Vice President Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo Leadership Award in 1997. She is currently a member of the St. Faustina Catholic Church Building Committee which is the fastest growing church in the Archdiocese of Galveston.
